About me

A potted history

I was born in a busy Suffolk town and my early years were spent living by the sea. My family moved to a small village in Norfolk just before I started primary school. We lived in the middle of a forest, which had many secret paths, babbling brooks, and wild places to explore. My time at high school was where I developed a keen interest in science - gaining an understanding of how the world worked, and I went on to a college to begin my science education in earnest. I decided to move up north for university - Durham, and pursued a degree in Natural Science but, as my full-time education began to end, I had a hankering for exploration. I spent several years exploring the world, while teaching English. I developed a passion for this career, and decided to combine both science and education. Having worked in education for many years, I felt that I was ready for a new adventure in computing, where I am able to combine all of my strengths: problem-solving, creativity, logic, science, maths, and focus.
